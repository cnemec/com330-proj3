YUME NIKKI

yume nikki itself

Yume nikki is a game developed by the japanese developer kikiyama using rpg maker. It was solely about exploring the dreams of the main character collecting effects on the way. The game plays by simply walking around exploring dreams connected through various points, occasionally using effects to pass, with a bird's eyes view.

While the concept seems simple it was revolutionary in the era completely innovating independent games forming what was basically its own genre. People were amazed by the pure creativity and expansiveness of this game, which came completely out of nowhere.

The game wasn't exploring just anyone's dreams though. Due to the consistent themes and layout of these dreams fans were able to expand on the story. Finding the simple looking game was hiding some pretty dark themes.

The dawn of fan games

Due to the amazing reception of yume nikki others were bound to be inspired. Yume nikki basically became it's own genre creating games like .flow. Taking the environmental and mental story telling of yume nikki and making its own story.

.flow has much darker dreams having the main character trapped in a hospital room instead of their home like in yume nikki. The setting is much more melancholy being a story about a girl dying of disease just trying to remember her life.

While .flow will always have a place in the community the biggest fan game is yume 2kki a collaborated effort of the entire fan community. This game while having less stories lets the player experience the dreams of the entire fanbase. Where people will have completely different experiences playing. And with the rise of walking simulators and exploration games the foresight of these games is rather impressive.

What this game lacks in themes it more than makes up for it in being an experience. You can really feel the passion in the whole community, their love for the series all coming together to make a game that could be a worthy sequel. That kind of love for a project always manages to come through to the player.

META NARRATIVE GAMES

OFF

OFF is a game created by martin Georis or Mortis Ghost as that is the pseudonym he used for the game, made in 2008. The story is rather interesting using its medium to affect the narrative.

The gameplay is that of a fairly standard jrpg with puzzles. Think of the battles as the original final fantasys and the puzzles as something out of professor layton. The camera is the same as yume nikki as the engine loses moddability in order to be more useable.

The meta element came from the name OFF. For one the game isn't downloaded as the game itself, you open a file creating the game. This plays to the theme of you being the one who caused the world to be created the way it is. As such it is your responsibility to fix it. By turning the world OFF

The expansion on meta games

one shot

One shot is the story of a wandering creature in a doomed world living on your pc. The inhabitants see you as a god and are trying anything to save their home. A lot comes up but due to the fact they are still selling the game I will avoid spoilers.

One shot is interesting due to the way it plays. The name and setting aren't just there to be weird if you close the game that's it, and the game makes files on your pc for puzzles. It takes advantage of what a game is and the setting to make a compelling narrative that complements the game.

Space funeral

Space funeral is extremely weird as if it is completely built around being strange. The characters are creatures like horses made out of legs or a boy that always cries, whose walking animation constantly changes proportions. The world and setting is odd, mean and oddly funny at points.

This all makes sense considering the ideology of the creator of the game. The creator hated the new trend of making pixel art games as if it is the only type of independent game. He wanted to make something completely strange in order to cause innovation. It used its setting as a game to allow a typical cliche world to be morphed into a completely original one leading to a strange and needless to say interesting story.

RPG MAKER HORROR GAMES

brief disclaimer

The horror game is definitely one of the hardest genres to get right, especially if the characters are small and the camera is zoomed out where everything is in frame. As such most of these games are not good, if anything most of them would make the player cringe before they induce fright.

Yume nikki inspiration

Some could argue that horror games in rpg maker are just inspired by yume nikki. While this is true the nightmares of yume nikki inspired creators to try to make these games scary I would say they aren't the same genre. While they came from the same roots they expanded into their own genres.

IB

IB is the story of a little girl, named IB, trapped in an art gallery, one that is slowly becoming hell pulling her in to teach her a lesson. Similar to silent hill in a way except obviously the little girl did nothing truly wrong to deserve being punished. Though now there the player has to run from monsters, solve puzzles and talk with other survivors to escape.

The story of IB is all about growing up, and how that causes people to see the world completely differently as they grow up. This can be seen in a few subtle ways only a game could show. Such as at the start of the game there are words that IB doesn't know but by the end if you read the text again she will understand the words. As well as seeing the game from the perspective of an adult and a child, things that would be cute and innocent for one of them will be dark and distorted for another, this has a real life parallel in porcelain dolls. There are many more examples of this but I will leave that to people curious in the games to discover themselves.
